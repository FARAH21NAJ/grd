Neurological

Neurological diseases encompass a range of conditions affecting the nervous system, including the brain, spinal cord, and nerves. Physical therapy is instrumental in managing these conditions by addressing impairments in movement, balance, coordination, strength, and functional abilities. Some cases

Some of the common sports-related diseases and conditions addressed by sports physiotherapy include:

1-Stroke (Cerebrovascular Accident, CVA): Physical therapy plays a vital role in stroke rehabilitation. Therapists focus on restoring movement and function lost due to brain damage caused by a stroke.

2-Multiple Sclerosis (MS): MS is a chronic autoimmune disease affecting the central nervous system. Physical therapy helps manage symptoms such as muscle weakness.

3-Parkinson's Disease: Physical therapy aims to address the movement difficulties, stiffness, and tremors associated with Parkinson's.

4-Spinal Cord Injuries (SCI): These injuries often result in paralysis or impaired movement. Physical therapy concentrates on strengthening muscles.

5-Traumatic Brain Injury (TBI): Physical therapists aid in restoring function, balance, coordination, and cognitive skills post-TBI. Therapy focuses on regaining motor control, relearning skills.

6-Cerebral Palsy: This condition affects movement and muscle coordination. Physical therapy helps manage symptoms by working on muscle tone.
