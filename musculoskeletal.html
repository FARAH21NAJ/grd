musculoskeletal

A The musculoskeletal system in the human body helps provide proper form and support,It contributes to the stability of the body, and forms an important part of the movements.This system is made up of several parts, including muscle tissue, cartilage, tendons, joints, and ligaments. The skeleton itself, along with additional connective tissues, forms part of the musculoskeletal system. Because of the role of the musculoskeletal system, injury or disorders affecting any part of it can lead to long-term complications. In some cases, there may be obstruction. Numerous musculoskeletal conditions have been identified in recent years, some more serious and disabling than others.

Some cases

Here are some musculoskeletal diseases and conditions commonly addressed through physical therapy:

1-Osteoarthritis: This is a degenerative joint disease characterized by the breakdown of joint cartilage and underlying bone. Physical therapy interventions focus on exercises to strengthen muscles supporting the affected joints.

2-Rheumatoid Arthritis: An autoimmune disease affecting joints, leading to inflammation, pain, and potential joint damage. Physical therapy aims to preserve joint function, In conditions like heart disease or post-heart attack, physical therapy focuses on cardiac rehabilitation,

3-Back Pain: Conditions like herniated discs, sciatica, or general lower back pain benefit from physical therapy interventions such as stretching exercises, core strengthening, postural corrections, manual therapy techniques,

4-Fractures and Orthopedic Injuries: Physical therapy is crucial during the rehabilitation process post-fracture or orthopedic surgery.

5-Muscle Strains and Ligament Injuries: Whether due to sports-related injuries or overuse, physical therapy helps in the rehabilitation process by guiding individuals through progressive exercises,

6-CTendonitis and Bursitis: These conditions involve inflammation of tendons or bursae, leading to pain and limited mobility.
