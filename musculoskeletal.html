musculoskeletal

A The musculoskeletal system in the human body helps provide proper form and support
,It contributes to the stability of the body, and forms an important part of the movements.
This system is made up of several parts, including muscle tissue, cartilage, tendons, joints, and ligaments. The skeleton itself, along with additional connective tissues, forms part of
the musculoskeletal system. Because of the role of the musculoskeletal system, injury or disorders affecting any part of it can lead to long-term complications. In some cases, there may be obstruction. Numerous musculoskeletal conditions have been identified in recent years, some more serious and disabling than others.

There are many methods that can be used in physiotherapy for the musculoskeletal system. These methods include

1- Physical exercises: Physiotherapy exercises are used to improve the flexibility, strength and ductility of the muscles and surrounding tissues.

2- Manual Therapy: It is used through massage and other techniques to reduce pain and improve range of motion and flexibility.

3- Heat therapy: It is used to relieve pain and improve blood flow to the affected area. Moist or dry heat can be used.

4-Cold therapy: It is used to relieve pain and reduce inflammation. .

5-Electrotherapy: It is used to stimulate muscles and reduce pain.
